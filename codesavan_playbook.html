Interview Script — Fast Screening (10 Minutes Max)

Keep it conversational. You're listening more than talking. Open with warmth, then let them talk.

Your Opener
"Hey [Name], thanks for applying — I saw your voice note and I liked what I heard. This is a quick 10-minute call, just to make sure we're a good fit before I send you your login and leads. Sound good?"
Q1

Background Check

"Walk me through your cold calling experience. What were you selling, who were you calling, and how long did you do it?"

→ Listen for: specific industries, US-based calling experience, appointment-setting language

Q2

Volume & Output

"On your best week — how many dials were you making per day and what was your booking rate?"

→ Listen for: 80–150+ dials/day = experienced. Below 40 = may be slow.

Q3

Confidence Test

"Tell me — without using the word 'um' — why a plumber in Dallas needs a website. You've got 30 seconds."

→ Listen for: fluency, no filler words, logical argument, calm delivery

Q4

Rejection Resilience

"How do you handle it when someone hangs up on you 20 times in a row?"

→ Listen for: humor, resilience, process-focus ("I just move to the next one") vs. emotional reaction

Q5

Tech & Availability

"What's your internet speed, do you have a headset, and are you available 8am–5pm EST?"

Red Flags — Reject Immediately

Heavy accent that makes them hard to understand on the phone
Long pauses, excessive "um/uh" in basic answers
Says they've never called US businesses
Unstable internet or no headset — non-negotiable for dialers
Can't answer Q3 confidently — if they can't pitch themselves, they won't pitch yours
Asks about guaranteed salary immediately — low commitment signal

Green Flags — Fast Track

Clear, confident English with zero accent friction
Has called US service businesses before
Laughs about rejection — they've been through it
Asks about the script, the niche, the offer — shows initiative
Mentions performance bonuses without being asked — competitive mindset

Onboarding Script

This is the most important conversation you'll have. How you frame the 5-day window and the $150 reward determines how hard they work. Make them feel like they just got access to something others didn't.

Onboarding Call — Word For Word
"Alright [Name], you're in. Here's how this works — I'm going to send you your JustCall login, your 500 leads, and the script today. Your job is simple: call through your list and book demo appointments for me to close.

Here's what I want to be upfront about — this first week is a performance test. Not because I don't trust you, but because I've learned the hard way that the only way to know if someone's a great caller is to watch them in action. Talk is cheap — results aren't.

If you book and convert 2 paying clients within 5 days — I'm sending you $150 cash, no questions asked. That's on top of whatever we agree for ongoing pay. It's my way of saying: you're legit, let's build this together long term.

Now — the 5-day window is real. We're on a free trial with JustCall and I need us moving fast. Every day counts. I'll be checking the Google Sheet daily, and I'm available on WhatsApp if you hit a snag with the script or dialer.

You ready to start today?"
// End with a direct yes/no question — creates commitment
Psychological mechanics at play: Scarcity (5-day window), reciprocity ($150 reward framed as a gift), social proof (implied others are competing), identity priming ("you're legit") — all without pressure or false urgency.

What to Send Each Caller on Day 1

  • JustCall login credentials + quick video walkthrough (use Loom)
  • 500 leads as a CSV (name, business, phone number, city)
  • The call script as a Google Doc (link, not attachment)
  • Google Sheet tracker link (view-only their own tab)
  • WhatsApp number for real-time support
  • One-page FAQ doc covering: what to say if asked about price, what counts as a "booked demo", how to log calls

High-Converting
The Cold Call Script

Core angle: "We already built your website. I just need 10 minutes to show you." This is a pattern interrupt — they expect a pitch. Instead you open with a gift. Never lead with price. Never close hard. Your only job is to get the demo call booked.

Step 1 — Opener (Pattern Interrupt)
"Hi, is this [Business Name]?"

[They say yes]

"Hey, my name is [Name], I'm calling from Codesavan — quick question for you: do you currently have a website for the business?"
// Don't introduce yourself fully upfront — a question disarms gatekeeping. Keep tone casual, not corporate.
Step 2 — The Hook (The Demo Reveal)
[If NO website]
"Perfect — so here's the reason I'm calling. My team actually went ahead and built a free demo website specifically for [niche] businesses in [City]. I built it to show you what your business could look like online — no charge, no obligation, nothing to sign. I just need about 10 minutes to walk you through it and get your thoughts."

[If they HAVE a website]
"Got it — I actually still built one for you because I wanted to show you what a modern, mobile-optimized version could look like versus what most [niche] businesses are running right now. It takes 10 minutes — worth a look?"
// Key phrase: "I already built it" — this shifts the dynamic from selling to showing. They feel obligated to at least look.
Step 3 — Book the Demo (The Ask)
"I can jump on a quick Zoom with you today around [time] or tomorrow morning — which works better for you?"
// Always give two options. Never ask "are you free?" — that invites a no. Force a choice between two yeses.

Objection Handling

"Just send me the link."
"I totally can — and I will. But honestly, the demo looks 10x better when I walk you through it and explain what we built specific to your business. It takes 10 minutes and I think you'll actually enjoy it. Does today at [time] work, or is tomorrow better?"
"I'm really busy right now."
"No worries at all — that's exactly why I want to schedule a proper time instead of keeping you now. What's your calendar like tomorrow morning? I'll send you a link and we'll keep it to 10 minutes flat."
"Not interested."
"I get that — you haven't seen it yet, so I wouldn't expect you to be. All I'm asking is 10 minutes to show you something I already built for free. If you look at it and don't like it, no problem at all. Fair enough?"
"How much does it cost?"
"That's a great question — and I'll be completely transparent about pricing on the call. But honestly, I don't want to throw out a number before you've even seen what we built. If you look at it and hate it, the price doesn't matter. If you love it, we'll talk numbers — and I think you'll be surprised at how affordable it is. Can we do tomorrow at [time]?"
"I already have someone handling my website."
"Totally fair — I'm not asking you to fire anyone. I built this as a comparison, just so you can see what's possible. A lot of our clients had someone before us too. Takes 10 minutes — worth knowing your options, right?"
"Is this a sales call?"
"Honestly? There's an offer at the end — I won't lie to you. But your only job today is to show up and look at a free demo we built. No pressure, no pitch deck, no contracts. If you like it, we talk. If you don't, no hard feelings."
Step 4 — Confirm The Booking
"Perfect. So I have you down for [Day] at [Time] — I'll send a calendar invite to your email right now. What's the best address?"

[Get email]

"Got it. You'll get an invite in the next 2 minutes with the Zoom link. Looking forward to showing you what we put together — I think you'll really like it. Have a great rest of your day."
// Always confirm day + time + email. Send the invite IMMEDIATELY while they're on the line. No invite = no show.

07
Psychology & Tactics
Performance Strategy
⚡

The Daily Leaderboard

Post a screenshot of the Google Sheet leaderboard every morning in the WhatsApp group. Top performer gets a shoutout. Bottom performers see where they stand. Competition drives volume.

MOTIVATION
🎯

Micro-Goal Framing

Tell callers: "Your only job today is 5 conversations that end with a demo booked." Don't say "call 100 people." Micro-goals feel achievable and build momentum.

PSYCHOLOGY
📞

Same-Day Follow-Up Rule

Any lead that says "call me back" or "I'm interested" goes into a priority callback list. Call them back within 2 hours — same-day follow-up 3x increases show rate.

CONVERSION
🔁

No-Answer Recycling

No-answers get called again on Day 3 at a different time. A lead that didn't pick up at 9am may pick up at 4pm. Most callers skip this — it's free extra inventory.

VOLUME
💬

WhatsApp Roleplay

On Day 1, do a 5-minute roleplay in the WhatsApp group. Play the prospect. Have them handle your objections live. Callers who rehearse perform 40% better in real calls.

TRAINING
⏰

Power Hour

Set a daily "Power Hour" — 9am–10am EST, everyone calls simultaneously. Create a group WhatsApp check-in at 10am: "How many calls? Any bookings?" Energy is contagious.

CULTURE

If a Caller is Lagging (0 bookings by Day 2)

  • Pull a recording from JustCall and listen together — identify the specific breakdown point
  • Do a live roleplay with them on WhatsApp voice note
  • Check if they're actually making the calls or just logging them — JustCall timestamps don't lie
  • If Day 3 = still zero activity, remove their leads and redistribute to top performers
